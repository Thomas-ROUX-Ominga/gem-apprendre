# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Three distinct audiences, all at a decision threshold:

- **Young adults (15–25)** who feel lost about their path — what to study, which specialties to pick, how to approach Parcoursup. They arrive anxious and leave with a direction.
- **Adults in career transition** — people who've hit a wall, want to pivot, or need to prove what they've already learned (VAE). Often stressed about starting over. They need reassurance first, information second.
- **Professionals seeking training** — people who want to level up in business creation, real estate, finance, communication, or sales. More autonomous, more transactional, but still need confidence in the provider.

All three contexts share one trait: the user is uncertain and needs to feel that this institution *gets them* before they act.

## Product Purpose

Gem'Apprendre is a Qualiopi-certified professional training center based in the south of France, founded by Cynthia Roux. It offers three core services: orientation counseling (for youth), skills assessment and VAE (for adults in transition), and vocational training (for professionals). The site's job is to take someone from "I'm not sure what I need" to "I have an appointment booked" — through the diagnostic tool, the service pages, and a frictionless booking funnel. Success means the user leaves with confidence, not just information.

## Brand Personality

Warm, expert, human. The feeling of a trusted advisor who happens to be a specialist: approachable without being casual, rigorous without being cold. Cynthia's personal conviction and regional roots (south of France) are the brand — not a logo or a tagline. The site should feel like she does: direct, bienveillante, quietly excellent.

## Anti-references

- **Generic HR/EdTech SaaS**: flat blues, stock-photo smiles, metric-dashboard layouts, "empower your workforce" copy. The opposite of personal.
- **Cold institutional**: government-style forms, no personality, black-and-white bureaucratic aesthetic. This is a human service; it cannot feel like an administration.
- **Cheap local directory**: inconsistent fonts, garish colors, cluttered layouts, visual noise. Erodes trust immediately.

## Design Principles

1. **Trust before persuasion.** The services are consequential and personal. The site earns confidence through clarity and credential, not through urgency or pressure tactics.
2. **Cynthia is the brand.** The founder's story, expertise, and warmth are not a "About" page — they are the value proposition. Every page should feel like her, not a faceless institution.
3. **Guide, don't overwhelm.** The user often arrives uncertain. Reduce cognitive load at every step: one clear question, one clear next action. The diagnostic tool models this perfectly — carry that principle everywhere.
4. **Credentials visible, not dominant.** Qualiopi certification matters deeply to the audience (especially for CPF/OPCO financing). Surface it with confidence, then get out of the way.
5. **Warmth through specificity.** Avoid generic "bienveillance" language. Warmth is expressed through concrete details: real stats, real testimonials, real outcomes from real people in the south of France.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG 2.1 AA minimum. Priority considerations: readable contrast for anxious or older users, no motion-first reveals that hide content, legible body text at ≥16px. The audience includes adults 40+ in career transition — assume no special tech literacy.
